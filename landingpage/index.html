StandUp! Research Project for Sedentary Behavior.

The human body is designed to move. For thousands of years, that’s exactly what humans did. In the mid-20th century, however, rapid technological advances (think: cars, TVs, computers, etc.) began chipping away at physical activity and people became increasingly sedentary. Technology has also influenced the way we work, most of us being tied to the chairs during our working days.
We believe that technology can be used to fight work-related sedentary behavior. We understand human sedentary behavior by developing an unobtusive IoT system for behavioural data collection and reasoning.
The research is conducted at the Computer Science department of Vrije Universiteit Amsterdam, combining the expertise of Behavioural Informatics and High Performance Distributed Computing research groups.
